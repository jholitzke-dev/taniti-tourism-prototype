Transport

Getting Around Taniti

Getting to and around the island is straightforward. Whether you prefer buses, bikes, or your own rental car, there’s an option to suit every traveller.

Arriving on Taniti

✈️

By Air

Taniti is served by a small airport that accommodates small jets and propeller planes. Almost all visitors arrive by air.

Airport expansion underway — larger jets within a few years.

🛳️

By Cruise Ship

A small cruise ship docks in Yellow Leaf Bay harbour once per week, staying overnight.

📍 Docks at Taniti City harbour

Ground Transportation

🚌

Public Bus Most popular

📍 Taniti City · 🕘 5:00 a.m.–11:00 p.m. daily · 💰 $

The most popular way to get around Taniti City. Cheap, reliable, and tourist-friendly.

🚌

Private Bus Rest of island

📍 Island-wide · varies by route · 💰 $$

Private buses serve areas outside Taniti City, including routes to the rainforest, volcano area, and more remote parts of the island.

🚕

Taxi

📍 Taniti City · available on demand · 💰 $$$

Licensed taxis are available throughout Taniti City. Ideal for late-night travel after buses stop running.

🚗

Rental Car Most flexible

📍 Island-wide · rent from airport agency · 💰 $$$

Rental cars are available near the airport and are the most flexible option for exploring the island at your own pace.

🚲

Bike & Scooter Rental Eco-friendly

📍 Taniti City & Merriton Landing · vendors open daily · 💰 $

Bikes and helmets are available from several vendors. Helmets are required by law.

🚶

Walking Free

📍 Taniti City & Merriton Landing · any time · free

Taniti City is fairly flat and walkable. Merriton Landing is also easy to explore on foot.

🗺️ Exploring on Foot

Many tourists find they need no other transport if they are based in Taniti City or Merriton Landing.

🚲 Helmet Law

Helmets are required by law when riding a bicycle or scooter on Taniti.
