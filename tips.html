Almost all visitors arrive by air. Taniti’s small airport accommodates small jets and propeller planes. There is also a cruise ship that docks in Yellow Leaf Bay once per week for an overnight stay. The airport is being expanded for larger jets within the next few years.
Entry requirements vary by nationality. We recommend checking with your national travel authority or the Tanitian Tourism Board before booking. Most visitors do not require a visa for short tourist stays.
Taniti uses the US Dollar (USD) as its official currency. Many businesses also accept euros and Japanese yen. Several banks facilitate currency exchange, and major credit cards are accepted at most restaurants, hotels, and shops.
Taniti uses 120-volt power outlets — the same standard used in the United States. If you are travelling from the US, you will not need a voltage adaptor.
The legal drinking age on Taniti is 18. Alcohol cannot be served or sold between midnight and 9:00 a.m.
Many younger Tanitians speak fluent English, and English is commonly understood in tourist areas, hotels, restaurants, and shops. In rural areas and among older residents, very little English is spoken.
Violent crime is very rare on Taniti. However, as tourism has grown, there have been increasing reports of pickpocketing and other petty crimes in tourist areas.

Taniti celebrates many national holidays. Many tourist attractions, restaurants, and shops may close on these days, so visitors should plan accordingly.
